Complex SVD with Real Matrices

The [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singular_value_decomposition, SVD] of a complex valued matrix, $C$, is $$ C=U \Sigma V^* $$ How can we compute $U$ and $V$ if only a *real* SVD is available (such as for [http://www.numericjs.com/, numericjs])? From [http://www.osti.gov/scitech/biblio/756121, this paper], with subscripts $r$ and $i$ denoting the real and imaginary parts, we know $$ K= \begin{bmatrix} C_r & -C_i \\ C_i & C_r \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} U_r & -U_i \\ U_i & U_r \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \Sigma & 0 \\ 0 & \Sigma \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} U_r & -U_i \\ U_i & U_r \end{bmatrix}^T $$ Here $K$ is an __equivalent real matrix__. Finding the SVD of $K$ also finds the SVD of $C$ (since, e.g., $U=U_r+jU_i$). In practice this can be coded as suggested [http://www.nr.com/forum/showthread.php?t=765, here]. (Note that the SVD is not unique and so the code may not recover the SVD exactly as shown above.)
